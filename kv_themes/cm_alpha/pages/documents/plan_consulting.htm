After coming to Los Angeles to work as a writer and director, I quickly realized that even though I had all the creative and technical skills needed to make a film, I knew nothing about the whole process that gets a film from an idea to a screen. I began educating myself through film production and entrepreneurship classes and was able to realize that there was an entire process that so many struggling independent filmmakers knew nothing about, and it was prohibiting them from moving their projects forward.

 

I began applying different business techniques and entrepreneurial models to the projects I was working on. I found out that the entire entertainment industry had become fragmented, leaving space for newer, younger, and more diverse talent to break in and shape the way the industry would work in the future.
